Step 1: Request Entry

The first step is to fill in a port entry notification. This notification will be sent electronically to the relevant authority to decide whether to allow you to enter port.

REQUEST PORT ENTRY 

Step 2: Assess Risk

Fisheries officers will then conduct a risk assessment to verify that your company / vessel / captain does not have any outstanding fines, infringements, violations and/or cases against it.

CHECK GOODSTANDING CHECK BLACKLISTS CHECK RECORD OF FISHING VESSELS

Step 3: Authorise Entry

If your vessel is cleared you will then be allowed to enter port.

Step 4: Inspect

Fisheries officers will then visit you at port and begin conducting their inspections on your vessel.

INSPECTION APP  INSPECTION APP 

Conduct Inspections

Use your mobile phone to conduct vessel inspections on fisheries vessels that are in port.

PHOTOS & GPS
Photos and GPS locations can also be taken and the data is synced directly to the cloud when the mobile phone is in range of an internet connection.

OFFLINE & CLOUD SYNC
This mobile application works offline and the data is then synced to the cloud when an internet connection is established.

Manage Data

The application can be used by multiple MCS Officers offline to manage information about vessel inspections.

As this data is synced to the cloud the information can then be synced back to all the other MCS Officers. That way all officers have a holistic view of the inspections that are going on in near real-time.

Map Data

The use of GPS location the mobile application helps MCS managers and analysts track where each inspection was carried out.

This is then plotted on a map so you know exactly when and where an inspection was carried out, who carried it out and what did they carry it out on.

Tally Catch

Use the application to tally the catch coming off vessels in port by species, date, weight, tally etc.

Photos and GPS locations can also be taken and the data is synced directly to the cloud when the mobile phone is in range of an internet connection.

Each unloading OR transhipment event in port is linked to the Port Entry ID generated when the vessel was requesting to enter port.
